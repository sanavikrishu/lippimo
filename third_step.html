Implementation of Habits

Habits are something that help us to solve problems. Basically, habits find the easiest solutions to our day-to-day problems. Like if we feel sad and have a Snickers, it instantly cheers us up. Then the next time we're sad, we immediately have a Snickers, as it is the easiest solution we are aware of. However, habits are very powerful too. They control your life. Habits define your lifestyle, your identity, and every part about you. To be consistent in anything, you can get habits. By getting habits, you'll always be able to do it every day. This is a key step to building systems and avoiding social media distractions. Reference: Atomic Habits.

To get a habit, you need 4 things: Cue, Craving, Response, Reward.

- Cue: Something that triggers the habit.
- Craving: Seeing yourself doing the habit with the result. For example, your cue might be seeing your dumbbells, and your craving is imagining your fit body.
- Response: The routine or behavior itself.
- Reward: Gives your brain dopamine and makes you want to repeat productive actions.

Cue

This is the trigger that starts your habit. It could be something in your environment, a time of day, or an emotion.

Craving

This is your motivation. You imagine the positive outcome or benefit of doing the habit, which makes you want to perform it.

Response

This is the habit itself, the action you take. It should be simple and easy to do to encourage consistency.

Reward

This is the payoff. It gives your brain a positive feeling, reinforcing the habit and making it more likely to repeat in the future.
